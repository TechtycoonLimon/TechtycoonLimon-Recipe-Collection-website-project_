Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, biryani masala, and salt for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat ghee, fry onions until golden, add cumin, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Add tomatoes, cook until soft, then add chicken. Cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Layer soaked rice over chicken, add 3 cups water, cover, and cook on low for 20 minutes.
  5. Fluff rice and serve hot with raita.

Bhuna Khichuri

Ingredients

  • 1 cup moong dal
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Roast moong dal until golden, rinse with rice.
  2. Heat ghee, add cumin seeds and onion, fry until brown.
  3. Add turmeric, dal, rice, salt, and water.
  4. Cook on medium heat for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve with fried onions and pickle.

Fuchka

Ingredients

  • 20 puris (store-bought)
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1 potato, boiled and mashed
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 2 cups tamarind water
  • Chopped coriander

Steps

  1. Mix chickpeas, potato, cumin, chaat masala, and salt.
  2. Poke a hole in each puri, fill with chickpea mixture.
  3. Pour tamarind water into each puri.
  4. Garnish with coriander and serve immediately.

Cooking Tips for Bangladeshi Cuisine

  • Use fresh spices: Roast and grind spices like cumin and coriander for maximum flavor.
  • Balance flavors: Bangladeshi dishes often combine spicy, tangy, and savory notes.
  • Soak rice: Soaking basmati rice for 30 minutes ensures fluffy grains.
  • Control oil: Use ghee or mustard oil sparingly for authentic taste without greasiness.
